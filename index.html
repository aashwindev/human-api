The four problems

You are a bottleneck. Your agents are blind. Your knowledge dies with you.

Three problems. Actually one problem — the absence of a queryable model of a person.

01 — Scale

You can’t be in ten places at once.

Founders, experts, parents — your judgment is your most valuable asset, and there is exactly one of you. Opportunity quietly leaks every hour.

02 — Agents

The agents acting for you don’t know you.

Every agent starts from zero. No taste, no voice, no context. MCP solved tools. Nobody has solved the human.

03 — Structure

Human knowledge is the most unstructured data on Earth.

Your company has a wiki. Your codebase has a graph. The actual you — the judgment, the lived experience — is not queryable anywhere.

04 — Decay

Most of who you are dies with you.

Letters survive. Decisions, frameworks, the way you actually thought — these vanish. Generational knowledge does not compound. It evaporates.

The product

A callable model of a person — built in six layers.

Not a note app. Not RAG over your documents. A structured, living graph of how you think — exposed as an API and worn as a clone.

L01 / Knowledge

What you know.

Your expertise, frameworks, beliefs and accumulated reading — extracted from journals, notes, emails, posts, saved articles.

L02 / Behavioral

How you actually are.

Your voice, your hesitations, your taste, your energy across topics. Inferred from video and audio — not transcribed, understood.

L03 / Temporal

How you have changed.

What you believed in 2020 vs 2025. The reversals. The hard-won lessons. The model is alive, not a frozen snapshot.

L04 / Relational

Who shaped you.

The people in your orbit and the nature of the relationship — so the clone speaks to your sister differently than to a Series A partner.

L05 / Skill

Native & downloaded.

Add bodies of knowledge — “learn all of Paul Graham” — and the system internalises it through your graph. Two people, same essays, two different outputs.

L06 / Interface

API & clone.

Exposed via MCP for agents. Wrapped in voice, chat, and video for humans. Same brain, two front doors.

The video breakthrough

The world transcribes. We extract behavior.

Hundreds of hours of personal video, voice memos and meeting recordings exist for almost every knowledge worker on Earth. The world converts it to text and discards 80% of the signal. The micro-pause before a hard answer. The topics that light you up. The way conviction moves across a sentence. That is the behavioural fingerprint of a person — and it is the only reason our clone feels like a human while everyone else’s feels like a chatbot.
